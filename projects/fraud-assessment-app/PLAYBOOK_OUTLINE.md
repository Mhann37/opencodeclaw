### 3. Verification Protocol (5-7 pages)

**"The 5-Step Wire Verification Checklist"** (printable, laminate-ready)

1. **Confirm the request came from a legitimate source**
   - Call the person directly (use phone number you already have, don't call the number in the email)
   - Verify identity before discussing any wire details
   - Example script: "Hi [Name], I received an email about wiring funds to [details]. Can you confirm this is legit?"

2. **Check the email details**
   - Look for slight variations in email addresses (check carefully)
   - Verify the sender's domain is legitimate
   - Red flags: Generic greetings, grammar errors, urgency language

3. **Verify banking information independently**
   - Call the title company/lender directly (use their published phone number)
   - Ask them to confirm the wire details
   - Don't use contact info from the email you received
   - Confirm account name matches expected recipient

4. **Review the transaction details**
   - Confirm: Amount, recipient name, account number, routing number
   - Check for unusual details (wrong amount, unexpected payee)
   - Compare to closing documents/contracts

5. **Authorize through proper channels**
   - Get written approval from broker/manager before wiring
   - Document who approved and when
   - Take a screenshot of the approval

### 5. Incident Response (3-4 pages)

**"What to do if fraud happens"**

**First 24 hours (CRITICAL):**
1. Stop all outgoing wires immediately
2. Call your bank's fraud hotline (don't wait)
3. Contact title company/lender
4. Document everything
5. Report to law enforcement (FBI IC3, local police)
6. Notify broker/owner immediately

**Within 48 hours:**
- Gather all communications (emails, messages, call logs)
- Interview staff involved
- Check all system access logs
- Change passwords/MFA
- Audit recent transactions

**Recovery steps:**
- Work with bank on reversal attempts (limited window, usually 24-48 hours)
- File formal complaint with FBI (IC3.gov)
- Consider hiring fraud recovery specialist
- Review insurance coverage

**Prevent future incidents:**
- Review and strengthen verification protocols
- Increase staff training
- Upgrade email security (DMARC, domain monitoring)
- Implement MFA company-wide

### 6. Red Flags Quick Reference (2-3 pages)

**Printable one-pager: "Red Flags for Real Estate Fraud"**

**Email Red Flags:**
- ⚠️ Sender email differs slightly from known contact
- ⚠️ Grammar/spelling errors in formal communication
- ⚠️ Urgency language ("must wire today", "don't delay")
- ⚠️ Request to use unusual payment method
- ⚠️ Generic greeting ("Dear Agent" instead of your name)
- ⚠️ Links or attachments you weren't expecting

**Request Red Flags:**
- ⚠️ Wire to new/unfamiliar recipient
- ⚠️ Amount doesn't match closing docs
- ⚠️ Banking details changed from what you have on file
- ⚠️ Request outside normal timeline
- ⚠️ Pressure to bypass normal approval process

**Behavior Red Flags:**
- ⚠️ Unusual urgency in normal communication
- ⚠️ Request bypasses normal people/processes
- ⚠️ Sender requesting secrecy ("don't tell anyone")
- ⚠️ Multiple people requesting same wire (possible group fraud)
